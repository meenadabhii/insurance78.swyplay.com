Insurance Buying Guide

Smart tips for choosing the right coverage at the best price.

How to Choose the Right Policy:

  • Assess your specific needs and risks
  • Compare multiple quotes from different providers
  • Check the financial strength rating of insurers
  • Understand policy exclusions and limitations
  • Review customer satisfaction and claims handling records

Ways to Save on Insurance:

  • Bundle multiple policies with the same provider
  • Choose higher deductibles to lower premiums
  • Maintain good credit (where permitted by law)
  • Ask about discounts (safe driver, security systems, etc.)
  • Review and adjust coverage annually

When to Review Your Coverage:

  • After major life events (marriage, birth, divorce)
  • When buying a home or vehicle
  • After significant changes in income
  • At least once a year during policy renewal

Insurance FAQs

Get answers to common questions about insurance coverage.

Q: How much life insurance do I need?

A: A common rule of thumb is 10-12 times your annual income, but it depends on your debts, number of dependents, and future obligations like college tuition.

Q: What's the difference between HMO and PPO health plans?

A: HMOs typically have lower premiums but require you to use doctors in their network and get referrals for specialists. PPOs offer more flexibility to see out-of-network providers but at a higher cost.

Q: Should I choose a high-deductible health plan?

A: HDHPs have lower premiums but higher deductibles. They can be a good choice if you're generally healthy and want to save on premiums, especially when paired with a Health Savings Account (HSA) for tax advantages.

Q: How can I lower my auto insurance premiums?

A: You can lower premiums by maintaining a clean driving record, bundling with other policies, increasing deductibles, taking advantage of discounts (safe driver, good student, etc.), and driving a vehicle with safety features.
